## 11.2 Academic Resources

### 11.2.1 Foundational Papers

**Seminal Works:**

1. **"Remote Exploitation of an Unaltered Passenger Vehicle"** — Miller, C. & Valasek, C. (2015)
   - The Jeep Cherokee hack paper
   - Demonstrated remote vehicle compromise
   - Foundational for modern automotive security

2. **"Experimental Security Analysis of a Modern Automobile"** — Koscher, K. et al. (2010)
   - First comprehensive automotive security analysis
   - University of Washington & UCSD research
   - IEEE S&P 2010

3. **"Comprehensive Experimental Analyses of Automotive Attack Surfaces"** — Checkoway, S. et al. (2011)
   - Extended attack surface analysis
   - Remote attack vectors identified
   - USENIX Security 2011

### 11.2.2 V2X Security Papers

**Authentication and Privacy:**

1. **"Security and Privacy in Vehicular Ad Hoc Networks"** — Raya, M. & Hubaux, J.P. (2007)
   - Foundational V2X security framework
   - IEEE COMST

2. **"SCMS: Security Credential Management System"** — Whyte, W. et al. (2013)
   - US V2X credential management design
   - IEEE VNC

3. **"Mix-Zones for Location Privacy in Vehicular Networks"** — Freudiger, J. et al. (2007)
   - Privacy-preserving technique
   - ACM WiSec

**Attack Research:**

1. **"Sybil Attacks and Their Defenses in the Internet of Things"** — Zhang, K. et al. (2014)
   - Comprehensive Sybil attack survey
   - IEEE IoT Journal

2. **"Illusion and Dazzle: Adversarial Optical Channel Exploits Against Lidars"** — Cao, Y. et al. (2019)
   - LiDAR spoofing attacks
   - ACM CCS

3. **"Robust Physical-World Attacks on Deep Learning Models"** — Eykholt, K. et al. (2018)
   - Adversarial attacks on traffic signs
   - CVPR 2018

### 11.2.3 Intrusion Detection Papers

1. **"A Survey of Intrusion Detection Systems in Vehicular Ad Hoc Networks"** — Sharma, S. & Kaul, A. (2018)
   - Comprehensive IDS survey
   - Computer Networks

2. **"Machine Learning-Based Intrusion Detection for CAN Bus"** — Kang, M.J. & Kang, J.W. (2016)
   - ML for in-vehicle security
   - IEEE ICVES

### 11.2.4 Blockchain for IoV Papers

1. **"Blockchain for Internet of Vehicles: Current Trends and Future Challenges"** — Mollah, M.B. et al. (2021)
   - Comprehensive blockchain survey
   - IEEE IoT Journal

2. **"BARS: A Blockchain-Based Anonymous Reputation System for Trust Management"** — Lu, Z. et al. (2018)
   - Blockchain reputation for V2X
   - IEEE TITS

## 11.9 Summary

This reference module provides a comprehensive starting point for deeper exploration of IoV cybersecurity topics. The field is rapidly evolving, with new standards, threats, and technologies emerging continuously. Staying current requires ongoing engagement with academic research, industry developments, and regulatory changes.

**Recommended Learning Path:**

1. **Foundation:** Read "The Car Hacker's Handbook" and ISO/SAE 21434
2. **Technical Deep Dive:** Study IEEE 1609.2 and SCMS architecture
3. **Regulatory Understanding:** Review UNECE R155/R156 requirements
4. **Practical Experience:** Set up a CAN bus test environment
5. **Ongoing Learning:** Follow conferences, papers, and industry reports
